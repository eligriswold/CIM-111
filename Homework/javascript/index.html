Step 1

You've just woken up. Your knee is sore and getting choked by the
brace you're forced to keep straight for 2 weeks. But it's time to rally.

How to Get Up

  • Kick the covers off of your injured leg with your healthy leg.
  • Gently lift your injured leg by holding the brace.
  • Land SOFTLY on the floor.
  • Stand up GENTLY.
  • Bingo! Proceed to Step 2.

Step 2

You're out of bed. Great. Now pop some Aleve and Extra Strength
Tylenol and grab those crutches.

Crutching Basics

  • Hobble to wherever you left your crutches.
  • Set them to the right length.
  • Balance your weight on your hands.
  • Blast off! Now you're moving!

Step 3

You got your crutches, you're out of bed, now comes the hard(er) part.
Crutch as fast as you can to the shuttle stop.

Shuttle Time! Excellent!

  • Your first time in the outside world, people will stare. Ignore them.
  • Crutch as fast as you can to the nearest stop.
  • Watch one pull into the stop. Race to catch it.
  • It left right before you got there, Ouch! Wait for the next one.
    • I didn't say you'd make it on time, gimme a break.

Step 4

After a significant struggle, you made it to campus. Time to treat
yourself!

Time for a Coffee Break

  • The trip too a lot out of you. Time to recharge.
  • If your shuttle stops at Miller Circle hit
    either the Richter or Shalala Starbucks.
  • Rest your arms and catch your breath.
  • Coffee break's over, get on with it!

Step 5

The final push. This is what you've been waiting for. One last test.

This Part Sucks

  • You thought people stared before? This is the belly
    of the beast. Ten times the people. Try to ignore the stares.
  • Stop every 2 minutes for a break. (crutches hurt!)
  • People will cut you off. Try not to swing a crutch at 'em.
  • Reach the elevator. Pray it's not full.
  • You did it! You made it to class! I told you the guide worked!

Bonus Crutch Safety Gallery!

Crutches can be your best friend, or your worst enemy. Here's how to make the best out of them.

First, make sure they are set to the proper length. They should not press up against your armpit, or you're gonna have a bad time.
Leave some breathing room, and place all your weight on your hands. Finally, Make sure to use proper footwear.
Flip Flops = Bad, Sneakers = Good. Follow these safety tips and the steps above, and you should be cruisin'.
